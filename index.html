About the Department

The Department of Applied Geophysics at IIT (ISM), established in 1957 during the International Geophysical Year, is a premier institution in India for geophysical education and research. With over six decades of excellence, the department offers diverse programs, including a three-year M.Sc. Tech., a five-year Integrated M.Tech. in Applied Geophysics, a two- year M.Tech. in Petroleum Exploration (in collaboration with the Department of Applied Geology since 1983), an M.Tech. in Earthquake Science and Engineering (introduced in 2015-16), and doctoral studies. Equipped with advanced geophysical instruments, a seismological observatory, and specialized laboratories in areas like geophysical inversion, instrumentation, rock physics, and coal geophysics, the department is recognized as a vibrant research hub through initiatives like UGC-SAP, DRS, CAS, and DST-FIST. With alumni holding esteemed positions worldwide in oil companies, mineral industries, R&D organizations, and academia, the department continues to lead in cutting-edge research and innovation.

Themes

1. Strong motion earthquakes

2. Seismic hazards, risk and mitigation

3. Strong ground motions and soil-structure interaction

4. Numerical analysis for performance-based designing

5. Innovative technologies and safety engineering of structures

6. Lifelines and resilience

7. Liquefaction evaluation

8. Para seismic devices

9. Geophysics and aided construction of engineering projects

10. Geology, groundwater and geotechnical risks

11. Remote sensing for strong earthquake mitigation

12. Impacts of strong earthquakes in mining, minerals, petroleum and other energy sectors

13. Impacts of Strong Earthquakes on Environment

14. Near surface stress-strain evaluation and geotechnical modelling

15. AI/ML based designing and smart health monitoring of structures

Tourist Attractions

Parasnath Hills

Parasnath Hills are a range of hills located in Giridih district of Jharkhand. It is one of the most important pilgrimage centre for Jains. There are a number of temples on the hills, some of them are believed to be more than 2,000 years old. Parasnath Hill is 81 km away from IIT (ISM) Dhanbad. By car one can reach in about 1 hr and 50 min.

Deoghar

Deoghar is a famous religious tourist spot in Jharkhand. It is primarily known for the Baidyanath Temple, one of the 12 Jyotirlingas of Shiva. Deoghar is 120 km away from IIT (ISM) Dhanbad. By car one can reach in 2 hr and 40 min.

Ranchi

Ranchi is the capital city of Jharkhand. A number of tourist attraction are there in Ranchi city and surrounding areas. Ranchi is about 152 km away from IIT (ISM) Dhanbad. By car one can reach in 4 hr and 15 min. One can also travel by Train.

Bodh Gaya

Bodh Gaya is a famous religious site associated with the Mahabodhi Temple (a UNESCO World Heritage Site), situated in the Gaya district of Bihar. Bodh Gaya is 207 km away from IIT (ISM) Dhanbad. By car one can reach in about 4 hr. One can also travel by Train.

Shantiniketan

Shantiniketan was declared as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2023. Established in 1901 by the renowned poet and philosopher Rabindranath Tagore and became a great center of art and culture. It is is 156 km away from Dhanbad. By car one can reach in about 3 hr and 15 min.

Purulia

Many tourists visit Purulia to witness the charm of the natural scenery. Purulia is home to traditional folk dance and culture like Chhou Dance and Jhumur Song. Purulia (town) is 63 km away from IIT (ISM) Dhanbad. By car one can reach in about 2 hr and 10 min.
